Page 2: Star and Delta Connections

This page explains the two most common three-phase load connections and shows how current and voltage are related in each one.

1. Star (Y) connection

In a star connection, one end of each phase is joined to a common neutral point. The other ends are connected to the three line conductors. This arrangement is common in distribution systems because it can provide both line voltage and phase voltage.

VL = √3 Vph IL = Iph
Important idea: in star, the line voltage is larger than the phase voltage by a factor of √3.

Example: If the supply is 400 V line-to-line, then the phase voltage is 400 / √3 ≈ 231 V.

2. Delta (Δ) connection

In a delta connection, the three phase windings or impedances are connected in a closed loop. Each phase is directly across the line voltage. Delta is common in motors and in some transformer connections.

VL = Vph IL = √3 Iph
Important idea: in delta, the line current is larger than the phase current by a factor of √3.

Example: If each phase current is 10 A, then the line current is √3 × 10 ≈ 17.32 A.

3. Summary comparison

Star

Has a neutral point.
VL = √3Vph
IL = Iph

Delta

No neutral point.
VL = Vph
IL = √3Iph

Use

Star is good for distribution and mixed loads. Delta is widely used for motors and transformer windings.
