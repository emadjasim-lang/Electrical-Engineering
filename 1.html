Voltage

What is Voltage?

Voltage is the electrical pressure that pushes charge through a circuit.

  • Unit: Volt (V)
  • Symbol: V
  • Measured by: Voltmeter
  • Voltmeter connection: Parallel
  • Examples: 1.5 V cell, 12 V battery, 24 V battery
Current

What is Current?

Current is the flow of electric charge through a conductor.

  • Unit: Ampere (A)
  • Symbol: I
  • Measured by: Ammeter
  • Ammeter connection: Series
  • Examples: fan current, motor current, charger current
Easy Analogy

Water Pipe Analogy

Voltage is like water pressure, and current is like water flow. A greater pressure causes more flow.

Important Formula

Ohm’s Law

V = IR
V = Voltage (Volt)
I = Current (Ampere)
R = Resistance (Ohm)
More voltage or less resistance gives more current.

Practical Skill

How to Measure DC Current

The procedure below follows the uploaded lecture content and keeps the connections correct.

1
Select DC current range.
Turn the multimeter dial to the DC ampere range.
2
Insert probes correctly.
Black probe to COM. Red probe to A or mA depending on the expected current.
3
Break the circuit.
Open the circuit at the measurement point.
4
Connect the meter in series.
All current through the load must pass through the meter.
5
Switch on the supply and read the value.
Important: Never connect an ammeter directly across the supply.

Correct Method

Ammeter in Series

The ammeter is part of the same path as the load, so the load current passes through the meter.
Incorrect Method

Ammeter in Parallel

This is wrong because the ammeter has very low resistance and must not be connected in parallel.
Clamp Meter

Clamp Meter Method

Some clamp meters can also measure DC current. Clamp only one conductor at a time.

AC Current

Alternating Current

  • Current changes magnitude and direction continuously with time.
  • It is commonly represented by a sine wave.
  • Typical frequency: 50 Hz or 60 Hz.
  • Sources: alternator and generator.
  • Examples: home supply, grid, industrial power.
DC Current

Direct Current

  • Current flows in one direction only.
  • Magnitude remains constant in steady DC.
  • Frequency is 0 Hz.
  • Sources: battery, DC supply, solar cell.
  • Examples: battery systems, chargers, electronics, DC drives.

Power Formula

Important Formulae

AC Power
P = V I cos φ

φ is the phase angle and cos φ is the power factor.

DC Power
P = V I

For steady DC, voltage and current are constant.

AC Voltage

Alternating Voltage

  • Voltage changes magnitude and polarity with time.
  • It is commonly a sine wave.
  • Frequency is usually 50 Hz or 60 Hz.
  • Sources: alternator, generator, mains.
  • Examples: home supply, industrial supply, generator output.
DC Voltage

Direct Voltage

  • Voltage has a constant polarity.
  • Magnitude remains constant in steady DC.
  • Frequency is 0 Hz.
  • Sources: battery, DC supply, solar panel.
  • Examples: 5 V charger, 12 V battery, UPS battery.

Measuring Instruments

Common Instruments

  • Multimeter: measures voltage, current, resistance, and more.
  • Voltmeter: measures voltage in parallel.
  • Ammeter: measures current in series.
  • Clamp meter: measures current without breaking the circuit.
Safety Tips

Safety

  • Use the correct meter range.
  • Never touch live wires or terminals.
  • Check polarity in DC circuits.
  • Use insulated tools and safety equipment.
  • Follow all electrical safety rules.
Incorrect connections may damage instruments or cause injury.
